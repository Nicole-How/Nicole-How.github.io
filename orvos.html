Orvos

June 4 — June 5, 2022 (24h)

Our project’s main focus for Hack The Woods, was on the communication between doctors and patients from appointment to appointment as it can be difficult and inefficient.

The Problem

Managing appointments and meeting multiple doctors/departments can be stressful and overloading for patients. Resulting in a negative overall experience.

MY ROle

Research, Ideation, UI/UX, Visual design, Prototyping, Powerpoint

Team

Myself, Roslyn Nip, Hailey Luong

Tools

Challenge

How might we simplify the process of filing Electronic Health Records to improve the experience of patients and healthcare professionals.

Our Solution

Orvos streamlines the before and after process of appointments between patients and healthcare professionals to make appointments quick and easy for both parties. By prioritizing the ability to exchange necessary information and acting as a hub for scheduling, aftercare, and next steps.

Home Page

The medical log acts as a timeline that both the patient and doctor can access for future reference. It logs any relevant events, past appointments, symptoms and or tracked data by the patient.

Patients can block out dates they are unavailable for scheduling, and select certain preferences to provide doctors and or administration more information towards the patients time based needs. As well as display dates already booked for other appointments.

Process

Research

Based on our initial research we pinpointed 3 key problems to focus on.

EHRs are often difficult to use and require training to operate. There is a need for an intuitive and streamlined EHR system that allow healthcare providers to better connect with patients between appointments.

Source

Weak IT security measures: The healthcare industry is vulnerable to cyber security attacks – the U.S. had more than 560 cyberattacks against healthcare facilities in 2020.

Source

Getting patients more involved: According to the national library of medicine, involving patients in the healthcare process provides patients with a better sense of control and responsibility in care activities — a leading cause in patient dissatisfaction is lack of communication with healthcare professionals.

Source

Task Flow

Given the limited time provided, we did a quick task flow and voted on which tasks to prioritize and began wireframing.

Low-Fi Wireframes

Our wireframes allowed us to experiment and figure out a consistent design style best suited for our primary users in mind. That was both aesthetic and accessible.

Visual Design

Orvos's brand was inspired by present healthcare visual connections. We built it around the colour blue as it calming and has been closely tied to credibility and trust between healthcare providers and patients. Our fonts are simple and bold prioritizing readability for all patients.

Reflection

Test & Validation

Due to the limited time constraint of the hackathon tests were not something we could do. However, if we had more time in order to ensure the quality of our design I would have done a diary study of about 4–6 month time span with a subject pool varied in ages 16–75+. This would test our design in a real setting with a varied user base as we designed the app to accommodate up to the most marginalized user young and old.

Team Work

With the limited little time we had and so much to do we all organized and worked hard in order to deliver quality work. Freely communicating with one another and understanding each individual members strengths and weaknesses allowed us to better quickly deliver a cohesive design and solid solution to our problem and win the hackathon together!
