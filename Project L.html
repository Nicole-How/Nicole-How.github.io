Project L Launcher

September 1 - September 16 2023

Project L is a 2v2 fighting game being developed by Riot Games. As an avid gamer, I wanted to explore UI/UX within the gaming sphere by conceptualizing how Riot’s new upcoming game Project L.

Summary

This is my concept for a desktop launcher user interface for Project L, an upcoming game being developed by Riot Games. This personal case study was designed before Project L's release and is unrelated to the official game owned by Riot Games.

Challenge

Considering visuals shared to date for Project L— what could Project L’s launcher look like? How can I draw inspiration from Riot's previous launcher designs and improve on other popular fighter games.

The Solution

Based on my research and visual analysis I developed a desktop prototype for the main menu , lobby for both single and tag-team, and story mode menu. Following a quick, snappy and dynamic visual style based on current Project L visuals (as of September 2023).

Desktop Prototype

Main menu

The main menu features the main navigation players will go through such as the play and story mode. As well as utilizes familiar key elements from Riot’s other games League of Legends and Valorant, such as the friends list and top management bar displaying the user’s currency and settings. (Note the gif will not play on the prototype when viewed on the browser)

Lobby Singleplayer

While Project L is meant for 2v2 duo play players can play as a solo tag-team fighter and control both champs at once. However players can swap between modes effortlessly through the navigation above their player card. Above the mode navigation players have condensed version of the main menu where they can even swap to story mode by toggling the “play” button.

Lobby Tag-team

In tag-team mode players can invite up to 3 friends for a custom match or just invite their duo to play against other duos looking for a fight. The layout is inspired by gameplay featuring the character selection, and is designed to smoothly transition the player cards into the character cards upon finding a match.

story mode

While story mode is not confirmed this is what I imagine a version of the story mode menu to look like. Players can read a synopsis of the champs and select which one to play through to learn more about their lore to unlock them for online mode.

Process

Research

To start I conducted a visual analysis on Riot Games branding, gameplay released for Project L (as of September 2023), and the UI/UX of their other titles such as League of Legends and Valorant. Using these insights, I created a desktop prototype for Project L's main menu, lobbies for both single and tag-team, and story mode menu. My goal was to maintain visual consistency with Riot's existing games and capture the quick, responsive feel of fighter games in the UI. I also studied similar titles like Tekken, Super Smash Bros Brawl, and Street Fighter to align Project L's UI with gamer expectations within the fighting game genre as well as taking note of what was failing.

sketches

Based on my research and visual analysis I listed the main flows to work on and began developing some layouts in my sketchbook. Considering visual hierarchy and what elements I wanted to have more focus within a given layout.

Icons

I decided to use icons inspired by League of Legends' current iconography, given that Project L draws its champions and storyline from the League universe. I made sure to adapt these icons to align with Project L's unique visual style.

Visual Design

Through my visual analysis I was able to grasp a good understanding of Project L’s branding and overall feel.

Reflection

Human centered Design

Taking into account the fact that the target audience consists of gamers, whether they are already familiar with Riot's products or not, I adjusted my approach to designing the UI. This involved aligning the design with the expectations and preferences commonly associated with gamers. This approach prioritizes meeting the needs and preferences of the target audience practicing human-centered design.

Consistancy and Simplicity

Consistency and simplicity in UI/UX design are crucial for making things familiar and intuitive. Simplicity reduces complexity, making interactions effortless, while consistency ensures predictability, relying on users' prior experiences to guide them. This familiarity accelerates user learning and enhances overall satisfaction, ultimately resulting in a more effective and enjoyable user experience.
