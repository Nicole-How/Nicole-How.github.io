Sprout Box

February — April 2022

Final project about current food production systems following the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDG), and York Capstone Network discussing urgent issues of food security and safety.

The Problem

The need for a sustainable food production system is higher than ever — in order to address food safety and security issues in homes, communities, and food services providers serving underprivileged groups.

Challenge

”How might we solve the inequalities within distributed food production systems and  provide food security and safety for homes  and communities?”

Our Solution

Sprout Box is a modular sustainable planter that is locally scalable and designed to one day become a globalized product to alter the way that farming, and locally grown food is provided to local communities.

Onboarding

Our app is about functionality, allowing users to manage all Sprout Boxes paired onto the account.

Home Page

Users will be able to view the status and important information such as when to change the water and the pH level of owned boxes and or favorited boxes. In order to make the app feel more organic and open we decided to use a floating button that would open a radial menu for our navigation — where users would hold down the button to see their options and swipe to the desired window.

System  Management

The system management is the main function of the app, allowing user’s to control individual or multiple boxes at a time by grouping them together. As well as add new boxes to their account through Wi-Fi connection.

Manual

The app features a manual that users can access at any time with a diagram of main parts, how to assemble their kit, and important info and managing the different components.

Marketplace

The Sprout app features a marketplace for users to trade, buy and sell items such as seeds, plants, and produce, equipment, etc. In hopes to foster a community of future sustainable planters!

Research

In order to address these issues we began to research more into problems surrounding food production systems and scarcity to gain a better understanding before we began designing a solution.

Over time we began boiling down on what we wanted to target most — being food production and distribution.

Brainstorming

With our research and understanding of current systems we we’re looking to solve this issue on a community based scale. Originally moving towards optimized community gardens. However, after some thought and discussion with our mentor at Hi-Garden we began scaling down further towards households, with the opportunity to move outwards on a community scale.

Personas

With a rough idea for our product and key information we created three different personas to assist us in identifying user requirements and pain points for our product and a web app + mobile prototype later on.

Rough Sketches

Based on our findings through our research and consulting with our peers we began drafting some rough sketches of a modular planter.

Final Sketches

After some more discussion and more optimization we decided on a final design that prioritized providing the ability to have effective food production per square meter, fit in smaller spaces, and provide users to have food diversity.

Final Product

Sprout Box is a modular planter with a mini hydroponic vertical farm and soil planter box, designed for a sustainable efficient compact farming experience. It uses a Nutrient Film Technique (NFT) hydroponic system, recycling nutrient filled water through the vertical shelving.  

To further reduce space and increase yield per unit area, we designed the hydroponics system as a vertical farm. The modular bookcase design allows them to stack up next to each other seamlessly letting users to mix and match sizes to fit an area.

Website & App

Alongside the planter we decided to design a website to house general information of Sprout, and an app to manage the product to make it easily accessible where ever the user may be. Here we created a simple system map of the basic essential functions for both website and app.

Low-Fi Wireframes

Based on our system map, requirements, and personas we quickly created low-fidelity wireframes to begin planning out our design.

Reflection

REsearch

This project was heavily informed by a lot of research on a very multifaceted issue with a variety of topics to look at. In order to design a solid solution we spent much time sifting through which parts of the issue we wanted to tackle the most and focusing our research on important information and research related to this part of the problem. This helped us to not only dive deeper into the issue itself, but ensure our group was on the same page and veer away from unrelated information.

consistency

Our design includes multiple working parts that required a consistent visual design in order to create a clean seamless experience across our web app, mobile app, and Sprout box unit. This was done by starting with a solid foundation for our visual identity after having a clear idea of our product and functional greyscale wireframes. Allowing the group to work in sync throughout the design process.
