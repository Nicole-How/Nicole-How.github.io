SDG Info Design

February — March 2022

The Sustainable Development Goals [SDG's] are a collection of 17 interlinked global goals set up in 2015 by the United Nations General Assembly.

The Problem

Organize and visualize five selected data sets from the secretary general SDG report of 2021 provided by the UN. Then display the data in a visual medium that would help viewers more deeply understand the information.

MY ROle

Research, Ideation, UI/UX, Visual design, Prototyping, Organizing

Team

Myself

Tools

Figma, Illustrator,  Photoshop, Excel

Links

Behance

Challenge

How do I organize and visualize a large amount of data while providing information and projecting a story? In addition, how could it be used to make more informed decisions and determine the next step to achieving these Sustainable Development Goals or SDGs?

Home Page

The landing page feature a brief introduction to the SDG’s to provide context and details about the origin of the information, before providing navigation to each data set.

Poverty By Age

The data is split into two demographics, youth and adults, and is presented as strip plots. The strip plot best depicts the fluctuations over time throughout different countries. Allowing users to compare and contrast different years progression to one another between youth and adults.

Species  Conservation

This data set utilizes a coxcomb chart to best display the scale between each country in total amount, across each category type.

Health worker Density

This data set utilizes an isotype chart on a map to provide a sense of location, and display the sheer scale of health workers each country had in comparison to others. As well as compare the amount of each health worker type within a country and or countries.

Women In Parliament

Looking over the data I noticed each country had an upwards trend, which was a key factor I wanted to emphasize — to show the increase over time of women in parliaments over the years per country. It is presented using a sparkline graph as it is simple to read, easy to understand and straight to the point.

Tariffs On Developing  Countries

To display the contrast of varying percentages across each section depending on the category it falls under, I utilized a heatmap. Allowing the user to draw their own connections between these categories such as why some have higher tariffs placed upon them compared to another. As well as what may have occurred during each year to allow for the placed tariff to drop or increase in comparison to the last.

Process

Organization

After selecting 5 sets to focus on I decided to then sift through and organize each set of data into a google sheet. In order to make the data more manageable and easier to understand, I decided to merge the data together based on continent. This also made the data feel more complete as it felt fragmented in the way it was broken up.

e.g — merging West, South, East, South East, and Central Asia together to make up Asia in general.

Selected Data sets
  1. 5.5.1 (a) women in parliaments
  2. 1.1.1 (c) poverty by age
  3. 17.12.1 tariffs on developing countries
  4. 3.c.1 Health worker density
  5. 2.5.1 (b) plant and animal species conservation

Rough Work

Once all of data was organized I began experimenting the best ways to visualize each data set in a way that compliments the data and emphasizes what is important.

Low-Fi Wireframes

Through sketches and mood boarding I designed the first rough layout for the website. Considering the user flow and how I may be able to implement and fully take advantage of digital interactions; in order to make the information engaging.

Visual Design

In order to best reflect the SDG’s I researched the styling and theme of the current branding guidelines provided by the United Nations Department Of Global Communications.

Reflection

Organizing data

The SDGs had a significant amount of data to pull from which can make it overwhelming, but it also made it harder for me to start visualizing. Organizing the data not only made this process easier but it allowed me to better focus on the message I wanted to project through the data.

Engaging interactions

Working with the data I not only wanted to tell a story through the data but to make it engaging for the user as well. With this project it allowed me to explore more creative ways I could use interactions to design an engaging experience that was both meaningful and generate intrigue.
