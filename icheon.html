Icheon Ceramics Festival

September — December 2021

The Icheon Ceramics Festival represents the art and beauty of Korean ceramics. Where visitors can learn about the history of Korean ceramics and the industry, as well as see the differences between modern-day and past ceramic techniques used.

Project Overview

Establish a visual identification for the festival and apply it to a large format poster, e-ticket and a program that will make use of the same visual identity. By exploring a number of distinctly different ideas/concepts, based on the information you know about the festival.

Poster And Visual Design Process

Research

To begin forming a visual identity for the Icheon Ceramics festival I wanted to know more about the city of Icheon as well as the techniques and main pottery styles created in Icheon. I spent a lot of time looking at the types of patterns and art styles depicted on the pottery themselves. As well as the process of created a goryeo celadon and joseon white porcelain by master craftsman.

Sketches

Based on my research I sketched out iterations inspired by the different styles and patterns I found

Iteration 1

Based on a popular beautiful circular pattern that uses two pots. It focuses on the material/makeup aspects of ceramics unique to Icheon and modern style of Joseon white porcelain.

1
2

Iteration 2

Inspired by the process and technique of creating ceramics — the lines that appear on the clay when spinning it. In addition to Korean colours red, yellow, and blue.

1
2

Iteration 3

This last iteration focuses on showing the variety of ceramic forms (pots, tea pots, cups, bowls, plates, etc). To move away from just showing the one form of ceramic and exhibiting the many shapes and functions there are to ceramics.

1
2

Development

After discussion and consideration I chose to move forward with my version 1 concept as I felt that the the pottery themselves have very unique intricate patterns special to Korean ceramics. That also displayed the craftsmanship of Korean ceramics within Icheon. However I did end up swapping the pattern/texture to something more fluid.
