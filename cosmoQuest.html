CosmoQuest

September 2023 - March 2024

CosmoQuest is an interactive application designed to facilitate learning and understanding of space exploration, discoveries, and technologies.

The Problem

Not many recognize that space technology and research have a profound impact on life on Earth. While traditional astronomical blog style posts cater to scholars, they can be challenging for those who lack prior astronomical knowledge to engage and connect with.

My Role

Research, Ideation,UI/UX, Visual Design,Prototyping.

Challenge

How can I simplify complex information to make it accessible and engaging for those lacking prior astronomical knowledge. While encouraging user interaction to foster connection with the content.

The Design

CosmoQuest is an interactive application designed to facilitate learning and understanding of space exploration, discoveries, and technologies. It offers a variety of educational resources and activities that would be suitable both at home and or learning spaces such as museums, classrooms, and workshops. Users can engage in hands-on experiences to explore astronomy endeavors and promote accessibility in learning about the cosmos.

App Prototype

View Prototype

Navigation

CosmoQuest features a dynamic map navigation system designed to enhance exploration and engagement. Each node represents a learning module, separated by category through colour. Nodes are strategically placed, with related modules grouped together, such as the Earth and International Space Station indicating linked modules and or closely related topics. Over time, the map would change to incorporate new space technology and research, ensuring new fresh content over the blog style posts.

Learning Modules

The learning modules are interactive segments within an innovative application, gamifying the learning process. They showcase real-world applications of space technology—in this prototype the learning module uses research I’ve done on the water recovery system and its applications on Earth as a case study. These modules highlight the benefits of space technology and research in a straightforward manner, emphasizing their global advantages.

Interactive Puzzles

Gamifying the learning process aims to create an immersive experience that resonates with users, fostering appreciation for the impact of space technology. By integrating interactive elements, it encourages exploration and engagement, inspiring curiosity and innovation in space research and technology.

Process

Research Question

At the start of this project I was an individual with and interest in Astronomical endeavors and looked to explore, where I eventually formulated this research question that guided me through this project.

“ Through the case study of Water Recovery Systems within the Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) onboard the International Space Station (ISS), I will demonstrate the global benefits of Space Technology and Research. ”

Research Findings

Through my research question I found a common misconception: that space exploration lacks relevance to life on Earth. However, I discovered that space technology and research profoundly impact our planet. Studying astronomy not only fosters a deeper understanding of Earth and the universe but also prepares us for potential threats. Moreover, space exploration drives technological and research advancements that directly benefit life on Earth.

Requirements

Using my research as a base and resource for my design application I began outlining the requirements of what my application should do.

AEIOU

UI then identified the AEIOU's of my application, ensuring that my design is user-focused and aligned with their needs and use cases. This process enabled me to develop cohesive functions and features, creating a narrative within the learning modules.

Inspiration

Inspired by visits to Science Centres, I looked to design engaging and informative experiences. Drawing from hands-on exhibits and galleries that nurtured discovery and fostered connections with visitors of all ages. By simplifying complex information and incorporating playful elements, the design seeks to cater to both children and mature audiences, sparking curiosity and encouraging collaboration. Additionally, as an avid gamer, I drew inspiration from my own game experience from beloved titles such as Dorfromantik, exploring various game mechanics to gamify the learning process effectively.

Wireframe Sketches

With my research and information organized, I started sketching wireframes and storyboarding modules. I began by zoning areas for frequently displayed content to ensure consistency and familiarity as users learn new mechanics and information.

Rapid Prototyping

As I designed new mechanics, I ensured to test them for clarity, interactivity, and enjoyment in the learning modules. Additionally, I confirmed that the games aligned well with the content.

Visual Design

CosmoQuest's visuals and branding are clean and simple, aligning with the application's goal to simplify complex information. Designed to be engaging for all ages, and dynamic enough to accommodate changing content.

Reflection

Test & Validation

Due to the limited time constraint of the hackathon tests were not something we could do. However, if we had more time in order to ensure the quality of our design I would have done a diary study of about 4–6 month time span with a subject pool varied in ages 16–75+. This would test our design in a real setting with a varied user base as we designed the app to accommodate up to the most marginalized user young and old.

Team Work

With the limited little time we had and so much to do we all organized and worked hard in order to deliver quality work. Freely communicating with one another and understanding each individual members strengths and weaknesses allowed us to better quickly deliver a cohesive design and solid solution to our problem and win the hackathon together!
