Challenge

How can an Augmented Reality (AR) app effectively engage regular bike riders and help them better understand their bikes while also teaching them riding techniques to enhance their overall riding experience?

Our Solution

Kivo's aims to enhance the bike maintenance experience for various biking activities. It focuses on simplifying key skills such as bike maintenance by utilizing augmented reality (AR) technology to visually educate users about their bikes, preventing tool misuse, and identifying errors during the fixing process. This approach provides maintenance guidance, addressing resource gaps, complex terminology, and the absence of an organized bike parts database in the cycling community, ultimately improving bike maintenance.

App Prototype

View Prototype

Home Page

The app integrates weather app and Strava APIs to display metrics and send maintenance alerts on the homepage. Page formats vary based on user experience level, with options for beginners or advanced cyclists. Weaknesses in knowledge are identified, prompting reminders or suggestions for educational modules, constituting a backend feature.

Maintenance

The maintenance feature in Kivo app aims to ensure bike safety and smooth rides by using data from Strava and weather apps to prompt users for upkeep. It educates beginners on maintenance and serves as an assistant and checklist for experienced cyclists, emphasizing proactive care to prevent breakdowns and costly repairs.

Repairs

The repair feature in the Kivo app enables users to identify and resolve technical issues using augmented reality (AR) and a guided tutorial. If the problem exceeds the app's explanation, users are guided to visit a bike servicing shop for assistance.

Learning Module

The learning module feature in the Kivo app is crafted to be user-friendly and straightforward. It utilizes an augmented reality (AR) function to personalize learning by identifying bike parts specific to the user's bike.

Modify

The modify feature in the Kivo app serves as a specialized tool for users, enabling them to search for compatible bike parts. It offers the ability to preview products within the context of their own bike, ensuring a personalized experience. Additionally, the feature provides an index of parts, their compatibility, and directs users to purchase locations.

Research

While engaging with Maddy, our bike expert, offered anecdotal insights, we chose an autoethnography to comprehensively capture and consider the viewpoints of casual or beginner cyclists. Our research involved both novice and casual cyclists within the group. Notably, our auto-ethnographic study focused on 4 individuals presenting as feminine and under 5’5”, revealing discomfort during and after rides. Despite being a minority, this demographic provides valuable representation of casual cycling experiences.

Reflection

Technological Capabilities

Effective implementation requires a thorough understanding of technology, especially when it comes to user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design. Exploring various AR applications provided insights into current practices and limitations, crucial for envisioning improvements in interaction design, especially regarding bike repairs within AR. Understanding the limitations—such as the awkwardness of augmented reality on phones—helped to direct the decision-making process. This highlighted how crucial it is to match technology capabilities with user interface and experience in order to maximize utility and usability in design.

Streamlining Information

Improving accessibility in the cycling space requires breaking down complex technical knowledge into easily understood representations. In order to guarantee user-friendly design, we concentrated on factors such as attentive spacing, appropriate font sizes, and colour contrast checks. Furthermore, we prioritized the usage of diagrams, photos, and interactive features in our design over explanations that were primarily text-based. When doing bike maintenance or repairs, this graphic method makes complicated topics easier to understand and more user-friendly, improving user engagement and overall experience.
